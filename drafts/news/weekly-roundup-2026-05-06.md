The biggest story this week is a cluster of new hardware announcements arriving just as the spring buying season heats up. Human Touch officially opened orders on the Super Novo 3.0, Kyota introduced a dual-mechanism 4D chair positioned explicitly as a value play, and Nouhaus showed restorative massage technology at High Point Market. Buyers who are mid-research right now have more options to evaluate than at any point this year, which makes knowing what to prioritize even more important.

## New Chairs and Launches

**Human Touch Super Novo 3.0**

Human Touch has announced that the Super Novo 3.0 is now available for purchase, joining the brand's premium Ascent Series. According to the brand's press release, this is a continuation and upgrade of the Super Novo line rather than an entirely new platform. Human Touch has not published full spec sheets through independent sources yet, so we are holding off on detailed comparisons until we can verify the roller dimensions, track configuration, and weight capacity figures directly. What the brand has confirmed is that the Super Novo 3.0 sits at the top of the [Human Touch](/brands/human-touch) lineup and carries Ascent Series positioning, meaning expect pricing consistent with the upper tier of their catalog.

For buyers already considering the original Super Novo or the Novo XT2, the 3.0 is worth a close look before committing. We will update our Human Touch brand page once verified specs are available.

**Kyota Konbi M728 DualPro 4D**

Kyota's announcement of the Konbi M728 DualPro 4D is the more interesting launch from a value-per-dollar standpoint. According to the brand, the DualPro uses a dual-mechanism design, meaning two separate roller assemblies work in tandem rather than a single carriage moving along a track. David Figler, EVP of Product at [Kyota](/brands/kyota), stated the goal was to "make dual mechanism, high-end massage technology accessible to more people than ever before."

Kyota has not yet published the track type or verified pricing through independent sources, and we are treating the spec claims as preliminary until the chair ships and we can confirm figures. That said, the dual-mechanism premise is significant: most chairs in the mid-range segment rely on a single roller head, and buyers who want full-spine coverage without stepping into the $6,000-plus tier have had limited options. If the Konbi M728 delivers on its positioning, it could be a meaningful entry point into [4D roller technology](/learn/roller-dimensions) without the price tag that usually comes with it. We are watching this one closely.

For buyers wondering whether a dual-mechanism setup actually matters for their use case, the short answer is it depends on whether you have tension that concentrates in both the upper back and lumbar simultaneously. A single-carriage 4D chair still handles that well; dual mechanisms add coverage breadth, not just depth.

**Ceragem Master V5**

Ceragem has launched the Master V5 in South Korea, described as an entry-level spine care device. The Master V5 represents a lower-cost tier for Ceragem, which has historically focused on its thermal massage bed technology rather than traditional massage chairs. Details on U.S. availability are not confirmed. If you are shopping the Ceragem catalog, the Master V5 is worth tracking but is not yet relevant for U.S. buyers.

**Nouhaus at High Point Market 2026**

Nouhaus used High Point Market in late April to unveil what it describes as restorative massage technology aimed at addressing sedentary lifestyle health impacts. The press release from the event does not specify a new chair model by name, and no pricing or track specs have been published. This reads more as a platform and positioning announcement than a product launch. Nouhaus has been carving out space in the ergonomic and wellness furniture segment, and High Point is where furniture brands speak to retail buyers rather than consumers. We will monitor for any specific product rollout following the market.

## Technology and Innovation

**Ceragem and the ISO/IEC Home Healthcare Standard**

Ceragem this week published an ISO/IEC standard designed to enable home healthcare devices to communicate with one another as part of a connected wellness ecosystem. For buyers, the practical implication is this: the industry is moving toward chairs and wellness devices that share data, potentially with health apps, medical monitoring platforms, or smart home systems.

This is early-stage infrastructure work, not a feature you will find in chairs shipping today. But it signals that brands thinking beyond the current hardware cycle are building toward connectivity as a differentiator. Whether that matters to you depends on your use case. Buyers focused on pain relief and recovery today should not wait for this to materialize. Buyers who want their chair to integrate with a broader home health setup in three to five years should track which brands are participating in standards development.

## Brand News

**Infinity Circadian DualFlex Wins IBT Editor's Pick**

The Infinity Circadian DualFlex has been named the International Business Times 2026 Editor's Pick for Best Massage Chair. Award framing from trade and media outlets should always be read with some skepticism, and this one is no exception. That said, the Circadian DualFlex has earned consistent attention for combining a [SL-track configuration](/learn/track-types) with a dual-roller mechanism at a price point that undercuts much of the competition. If you are comparing it to other Infinity models or mid-range 4D chairs, the recognition is consistent with what hands-on testers have generally found.

**Bodyfriend Fined by Korea FTC**

South Korea's Fair Trade Commission fined Bodyfriend 40 million won for failing to issue written subcontract documents as required under Korean fair trade law. This is an administrative and compliance matter related to Bodyfriend's domestic supply chain practices, not a product safety issue. Three separate Korean news outlets covered the ruling this week. For U.S. buyers considering Bodyfriend chairs, this is worth noting as a signal of operational friction at the brand, though it does not have a direct bearing on product quality or warranty service in the U.S. market. We will continue monitoring.

**Human Touch on Chiropractor Perspectives**

Human Touch published a blog post surveying what chiropractors think about massage chairs as a complement to spinal care. The brand's own content is not an independent source, but the underlying question is one buyers ask us regularly. The short version: most chiropractors view massage chairs favorably as a tool for extending the soft-tissue benefits of an adjustment between office visits. They are not a substitute for clinical care, but for buyers managing chronic tension, herniated disc recovery, or postural issues, a chair with a well-calibrated [SL-track](/learn/track-types) and adjustable intensity can meaningfully support a chiropractic regimen. Use [our Chair Finder](/finder) if you want a recommendation filtered by pain profile.

**Family Month Promotional Competition in Asia**

The Korean massage chair market enters its peak season in May, known in the industry as Family Month. Ceragem, Body Friend, and Kojima have each launched promotional campaigns with notably different strategies, according to Chosunbiz. This is primarily a regional market story, but it is worth tracking: promotional frameworks that prove effective in the Korean market often inform how brands approach holiday and seasonal sales in the U.S. Buyers who are timing a purchase around Memorial Day or summer sales events may see some of the same competitive pricing pressure show up here.

**Consumer Reports: Best Chair Massagers of 2026**
Consumer Reports published its lab-tested roundup of the best chair massagers this week. It is important to note that Consumer Reports is testing massage pads and seat attachments, not full massage chairs in the $2,000-plus category. If you are comparing a seat pad to a full chair, they are solving different problems. A pad is a low-commitment entry point; a full chair with a [body-scanning roller system](/learn/roller-dimensions) delivers a fundamentally different therapeutic experience.
