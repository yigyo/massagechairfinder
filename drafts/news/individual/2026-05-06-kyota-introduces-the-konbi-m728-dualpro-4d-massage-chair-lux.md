Kyota has announced the Konbi M728 DualPro 4D, a new massage chair the brand describes as bringing dual-mechanism, high-end massage technology to a broader range of buyers. The announcement was made by David Figler, EVP of Product at Kyota Massage Chairs, and positions the Konbi M728 as a value-forward entry into a segment that has historically carried premium pricing.

## What Was Announced

According to Kyota, the Konbi M728 DualPro 4D is built around a dual-mechanism design, meaning the chair uses two independent massage systems working in tandem rather than a single roller track. Kyota describes this as the defining feature of the chair and the primary reason it believes the Konbi M728 represents unusual value in its class.

The "4D" designation indicates that, according to Kyota, the roller heads can move in four directions including an in-and-out dimension that allows the chair to vary massage pressure and depth dynamically, rather than relying purely on fixed roller movement. Kyota is framing the Konbi M728 as a luxury-feature chair at a price point designed to reach buyers who have previously been priced out of dual-mechanism options.

Specific pricing, confirmed dimensions, and full feature specifications were not detailed in the available announcement materials at the time of publication.

## What This Means for Buyers

The most meaningful claim here is the dual-mechanism approach. In the massage chair market, most chairs at mainstream price points use a single roller track, typically either an L-track that curves from the neck down through the glutes, or an S-track that follows the spine's natural curve but stops at the lower back. Dual-mechanism chairs add a second independent roller system, which can allow simultaneous massage of different body regions or more nuanced targeting of problem areas that a single track cannot reach efficiently.

If Kyota's pricing holds to what "accessible" typically means in their product lineup, the Konbi M728 could position itself in the $4,000 to $6,000 range, though that is not confirmed. Buyers in that window currently have strong competition from well-established [L-track chairs](/reviews/best-l-track-massage-chairs) and [4D massage chairs](/reviews/best-4d-massage-chairs) that have gone through more buyer feedback cycles.

The target buyer for the Konbi M728, based on Kyota's framing, is likely someone who has researched dual-mechanism technology and wants it without paying the $7,000 or higher prices that chairs with similar architecture have historically commanded. This could be a compelling fit for buyers dealing with both upper-body tension and lower-back or hip issues simultaneously, since dual mechanisms can theoretically address both zones in a single session more effectively than a single track.

Buyers focused purely on a long L-track coverage for lower-body issues, or those prioritizing zero-gravity recline and body scan precision above all else, should compare the Konbi M728 carefully against [top-rated L-track options](/reviews/best-l-track-massage-chairs) before committing. Dual-mechanism designs add complexity, and more complexity is only a benefit if it translates to a noticeably better massage experience for your specific body and problem areas.

## What We Do Not Know Yet

Several key details are not confirmed in the available announcement:

- **Confirmed retail price.** Kyota has described the Konbi M728 as accessible and value-oriented, but no specific price has been published in the materials available to us. This is the single most important unknown for buyers trying to evaluate the chair competitively.
- **Full specification sheet.** Roller dimensions, airbag placement and count, heat zones, weight capacity, and user height range are all pending verification. We will not report figures we cannot attribute to Kyota directly.
- **Availability date and retail channels.** The announcement does not specify when the chair ships or where it will be sold.
- **Independent performance data.** This is a launch announcement. No third-party testing or extended buyer feedback exists yet.

Specifications are pending verification across most of the technical categories that matter most in a chair comparison.

## How It Compares

Without confirmed pricing, direct comparisons are difficult to lock in. That said, it is worth framing where the Konbi M728 sits conceptually against chairs we have evaluated.

The [Daiwa Supreme Hybrid](/reviews/daiwa-supreme-hybrid) is one of the most well-known dual-mechanism chairs on the market, combining an S-track upper roller with a separate lower-body mechanism for a more comprehensive full-body experience. It carries a price tag well above what Kyota is suggesting for the Konbi M728. If Kyota genuinely delivers a comparable dual-mechanism experience at a lower price, that would be a meaningful development for buyers who have been watching the Daiwa Supreme Hybrid but found it beyond their budget.

For buyers comparing the Konbi M728 against a strong single-mechanism 4D alternative, the [Osaki Maestro LE 2.0](/reviews/osaki-maestro-le-2) is a relevant benchmark. It offers a long L-track, robust 4D roller control, and broad body coverage through a single but well-engineered system. Buyers should weigh whether dual mechanisms genuinely improve their massage experience over a mature, refined single-track design.

The Kyota Konbi M728 DualPro 4D is worth watching, particularly if pricing confirms the value positioning Kyota is signaling.
