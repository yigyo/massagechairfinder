Kyota has announced the Konbi M728 DualPro 4D, a new massage chair the brand describes as bringing dual-mechanism, high-end technology to a broader audience. The announcement was made by David Figler, EVP of Product at Kyota Massage Chairs, with the launch originating from the company's Seabrook, NH base of operations.

## What Was Announced

According to Kyota, the Konbi M728 DualPro 4D is built around a dual massage mechanism design, which the brand positions as a technology previously associated only with top-tier, high-cost chairs. Figler stated directly in the announcement: "With the Konbi DualPro, we wanted to make dual mechanism, high-end massage technology accessible to more people than ever before."

Beyond that framing, Kyota has not yet released a full public spec sheet through this announcement. The "4D" designation indicates the roller system is designed to move in multiple axes, including in-and-out depth variation, which allows for more nuanced pressure control than standard 3D systems. The "DualPro" name strongly implies two independent roller mechanisms working simultaneously, though Kyota's precise implementation of this has not been independently verified. Specifications are pending verification.

## What This Means for Buyers

Dual-mechanism chairs represent one of the more meaningful recent advances in the massage chair category. In a single-mechanism chair, one roller carriage travels the length of the track, covering the spine in sequence. A dual-mechanism setup runs two independent carriages, which can work simultaneously on different zones, potentially offering a more thorough and time-efficient session.

Until now, chairs with true dual-mechanism designs have generally sat at the upper end of the market, often above $6,000 and in some cases considerably higher. If Kyota has genuinely brought this technology down into the mid-tier range, that matters for buyers who have been weighing whether the feature is worth a significant price premium.

The 4D roller designation adds another layer of interest. Buyers dealing with chronic back tension or muscle knots often benefit most from 4D systems because the variable depth allows the chair to apply targeted, kneading pressure rather than a fixed-intensity pass. If you are comparing chairs for therapeutic use on a specific problem area, 4D plus dual mechanisms is a combination worth evaluating carefully. ## What We Do Not Know Yet

Several important details are not yet confirmed:

- **Price.** Kyota has not publicly announced a retail price for the M728 DualPro 4D. The "accessible" framing from the brand is notable but unverified until a number is attached to it. A chair described as accessible by one brand may still sit at $4,500 or $5,500, which is accessible relative to $8,000 competitors but still a significant purchase.
- **Track type.** The announcement does not specify whether the M728 uses an L-track, SL-track, or straight S-track. This matters considerably for buyers with lower back or glute tension, as L-track and SL-track designs extend coverage further down the body.
- **Availability and ship date.** No confirmed in-stock date has been provided.
- **Full specification sheet.** Airbag coverage, body scan type, heat zones, zero-gravity recline positions, and chair dimensions have not been confirmed in this announcement.
