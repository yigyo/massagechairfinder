Fujiiryoki announced the Cyber Relax AI in October 2024, positioning it as the brand's flagship intelligent massage chair and a showcase for what the company calls the future of adaptive wellness technology. The chair was unveiled out of the brand's North American base in Torrance, California, and represents Fujiiryoki's most direct push into the AI-driven premium segment.

## What Was Announced

According to Fujiiryoki, the Cyber Relax AI is built around two core ideas: AI-powered body scanning and adaptive massage algorithms that adjust the session in real time based on what the scan detects. The brand describes itself as a global leader in wellness technology, and the Cyber Relax AI appears to be the product meant to back that claim in the competitive upper tier of the massage chair market.

Fujiiryoki states that the chair uses intelligent scanning to map the user's body before a session begins, then applies that data to customize roller position, pressure, and technique throughout the massage. The brand frames this as a meaningful step beyond preset programs, arguing that the chair responds to the individual rather than simply running a fixed sequence.

Full specifications, confirmed pricing, and a detailed feature breakdown were not included in the initial announcement materials available at the time of this writing.

## What This Means for Buyers

The phrase "AI massage chair" gets used loosely across the industry, so it is worth separating what is genuinely useful from what is marketing vocabulary.

Body scanning itself is not new. Chairs at the [mid-to-upper price range](/massage-chairs) have used shoulder detection and spinal mapping for years to set roller starting positions. What matters is the quality of that scan and what the chair actually does with the data. A basic scan that only sets shoulder height is a very different thing from a system that continuously adjusts roller depth and speed mid-session based on tension feedback.

When Fujiiryoki says the Cyber Relax AI uses "adaptive massage algorithms," the buyer question is: adaptive in what way, and triggered by what input? If the chair uses pressure sensors to detect muscle resistance and respond by modulating intensity, that is genuinely useful for people with asymmetrical tension patterns or chronic tightness in specific zones. If "adaptive" means the chair selects from preset programs based on an initial scan, that is a more modest claim.

For buyers who have been researching the [premium segment](/massage-chairs/luxury), Fujiiryoki is a credible name. The brand has been manufacturing massage chairs in Japan since 1954 and has a long track record in the domestic Japanese market, where standards for build quality and engineering tend to be high. The Cyber Relax AI appears positioned to compete with other flagship chairs in the upper tier, likely above the $5,000 range based on the brand's existing lineup, though confirmed pricing is pending.

The most likely buyer for a chair like this is someone who has already decided they want a top-tier experience and is specifically interested in personalization rather than simply a long feature list. If you have asymmetrical posture issues, recover from athletic activity, or have found that preset programs never quite hit the right spots, intelligent scanning is a genuinely meaningful differentiator. If you are primarily looking for deep tissue pressure or a specific track type, the AI framing may be less relevant to your decision than the underlying hardware. ## What We Do Not Know Yet

Several important details are not yet confirmed from the available announcement:

- **Confirmed retail price.** Fujiiryoki has not published North American pricing in the materials available for this article. Given the brand's positioning and the flagship nature of this model, the chair likely sits in the upper premium range, but buyers should not plan a budget around that assumption.
- **Track type.** Whether the Cyber Relax AI uses an S-track, L-track, or SL-track configuration is not confirmed in the source materials. Track type is one of the most consequential specs for buyers with lower back or glute tension, and it will significantly affect how this chair compares to alternatives.
- **Full specification sheet.** Roller dimensions, airbag placement, height and weight accommodation, and zero gravity positioning details are all pending verification.
- **U.S. availability timeline.** The announcement was made in late 2024. Whether the chair is currently shipping to U.S. customers or remains in a preview phase is not confirmed.
- **Warranty and service terms.** Fujiiryoki has offered solid warranty coverage on past models, but terms for the Cyber Relax AI have not been published.

Specifications are pending verification across most of the technical categories buyers care about most.

## How It Compares

Without confirmed specs and pricing, direct comparisons require some caution, but the competitive landscape in intelligent and adaptive massage chairs is worth understanding.

The [Inada Dreamwave](/massage-chairs/inada-dreamwave) is the most natural point of comparison for buyers considering Fujiiryoki. Inada is the other major Japanese heritage brand with a strong presence in the North American premium market, and its flagship chairs similarly emphasize whole-body technique and build quality over raw feature counts. Buyers who are weighing the Cyber Relax AI should look closely at how both brands handle shoulder and lumbar scanning, since that is where the session quality difference tends to show up in real use.

The [Panasonic MA73](/massage-chairs/panasonic) also competes in the intelligent scan category and has a long engineering lineage in the Japanese market. Panasonic's approach to body mapping has been among the more technically substantive in the segment, and it sets a meaningful benchmark for what "intelligent" scanning should deliver at premium prices.

Both comparisons will become more useful once Fujiiryoki confirms pricing and track specifications for the Cyber Relax AI.

The Cyber Relax AI represents Fujiiryoki putting its most credible engineering claim, adaptive intelligence, front and center in a competitive market.
