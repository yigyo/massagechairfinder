Infinity Massage Chairs is debuting a new 2-in-1 power lounger at a Las Vegas furniture market, according to a report from Furniture Today. The product blends powered reclining furniture with massage functionality, positioning it as a living-room-friendly alternative to traditional massage chairs. A full launch date and pricing have not yet been publicly confirmed.

## What Was Announced

According to the Furniture Today report, Infinity is introducing a product it describes as a 2-in-1 power lounger. The "2-in-1" framing suggests the chair is designed to function both as a standard power recliner and as a massage chair, though Infinity has not released detailed specifications as of this writing. The Las Vegas furniture market is a major venue where brands introduce products aimed at mainstream furniture retail channels, which signals Infinity may be targeting a broader retail footprint with this product beyond its traditional massage chair specialty retail base.

## What This Means for Buyers

The lounger-massage hybrid category has grown steadily as buyers look for chairs that fit living room aesthetics without the bulky, clinical look of a dedicated massage chair. That is a real need. Many buyers who would benefit from a quality massage chair hesitate because traditional models can be large, visually prominent, and difficult to place in shared living spaces.

However, buyers shopping in this space should understand the trade-offs clearly.

A dedicated massage chair in the $3,000 to $6,000 range, such as Infinity's own [Infinity Luminary](/chairs/infinity-luminary) or comparable models from competitors like [Osaki](/brands/osaki) and [Daiwa](/brands/daiwa), typically offers a more sophisticated roller mechanism, a longer SL-track or L-track, and more targeted therapeutic programming than a hybrid lounger. The engineering priorities in a dedicated massage chair are built around the massage system first. In a hybrid power lounger, the priorities are split between furniture performance and massage function, which often means the massage component is less capable.

That said, for buyers who want something that blends into a living room, hosts guests comfortably, and still delivers a reasonable back massage, a 2-in-1 concept can make sense. The key question is whether the massage system uses a true roller mechanism with body-scanning capability or relies on vibration pads, which are far less effective for therapeutic use.

The Las Vegas furniture market introduction also suggests Infinity may be pricing this product to appeal to furniture buyers rather than dedicated massage chair buyers. That could mean a lower price point than Infinity's flagship chairs, or it could mean the chair lands in retail furniture stores alongside recliners from brands that have no massage chair expertise.

Buyers who are primarily motivated by massage therapy outcomes should [compare dedicated massage chairs](/compare) before committing to a hybrid. Buyers whose primary obstacle has been aesthetics or living room fit may find this worth a closer look once full details are available.

## How It Compares

Without confirmed specifications, a direct feature-by-feature comparison is not possible. What we can do is frame the category decision.

Infinity's own [Infinity Luminary](/chairs/infinity-luminary) represents what a dedicated Infinity massage chair looks like at a premium price point, with a full SL-track roller system and targeted therapeutic programming. If the 2-in-1 power lounger lands below that price tier, buyers should ask whether the reduction in price reflects a reduction in massage capability, a reduction in furniture quality, or both.

In the broader hybrid furniture-massage category, [Human Touch](/brands/human-touch) has historically occupied a similar position, designing chairs that are more living-room-friendly than traditional massage chairs. Human Touch products tend to emphasize aesthetics and usability for multiple users in a household, which is a reasonable comparison point for what Infinity appears to be pursuing here.
