Human Touch announced the Super Novo 3.0 on March 31, 2026, adding a new model to its premium Ascent Series lineup. The Long Beach, California brand positioned the chair as the next evolution of its flagship Super Novo platform, which has been one of the more recognizable names in the upper tier of the massage chair market. The chair is available for purchase now, according to Human Touch.

## What Was Announced

According to Human Touch, the Super Novo 3.0 joins the Ascent Series, the brand's premium product tier. The announcement describes Human Touch as "a leader in premium massage chair technology and high-performance wellness," framing the new model as an expansion of an already-established line rather than a ground-up redesign. Beyond that, the press release summary available at the time of publication does not include a detailed breakdown of new features, specific roller technology, or upgraded components relative to the original Super Novo. Human Touch has not published a side-by-side specification comparison between the 3.0 and its predecessors in the materials reviewed for this article.

Specifications are pending verification.

## What This Means for Buyers

The Super Novo name carries real weight in the premium segment. The original [Human Touch Super Novo](/chairs/human-touch-super-novo) was one of the brand's most ambitious chairs, featuring an L-track design and 3D roller technology aimed at buyers who wanted a sophisticated full-body massage without the clinical look common to Japanese-market imports. A "3.0" revision on that foundation suggests iterative refinement rather than a category shift, which matters to two groups of buyers.

First, anyone who already considered the Super Novo but held off has a reason to take another look. If the 3.0 addresses complaints about the prior model, such as session customization or body-scan accuracy, it could resolve the hesitations that pushed some buyers toward competitors. Second, buyers who are currently evaluating the original Super Novo or earlier revisions should pause before committing. Purchasing a previous version when a newer model is available at a similar price point rarely makes sense unless there is a significant discount involved.

For buyers drawn to Human Touch specifically because of its domestic brand presence, wellness-focused design aesthetic, and integration of features like cloud touch acupressure, the 3.0 will likely feel like a natural upgrade path. Human Touch chairs tend to appeal to buyers who prioritize a furniture-forward look and brand support infrastructure over the maximum roller intensity you might find from brands competing purely on massage performance metrics.

At the premium tier where the Ascent Series lives, buyers should also be cross-shopping chairs like the [Osaki Maestro LE](/chairs/osaki-maestro-le) and [Daiwa Supreme Hybrid](/chairs/daiwa-supreme-hybrid), both of which compete on track coverage and roller sophistication. The Super Novo 3.0 will need to demonstrate clear advantages in either massage quality, app integration, or design to justify its position against those alternatives. ## What We Do Not Know Yet

Several key details are not confirmed in the materials available for this article.

**Pricing** has not been announced in the summary provided. The original Super Novo launched at a price point that placed it firmly in the upper-mid to premium range of the market. Whether the 3.0 carries a price increase, holds steady, or launches at a promotional rate is unknown.

**Specific new features** distinguishing the 3.0 from previous Super Novo versions have not been detailed. Without a verified spec sheet, it is not possible to confirm whether changes involve the roller mechanism, track type, airbag configuration, or software and app functionality.

**Availability by channel** is also unclear. Human Touch sells through a mix of its own website and retail partners, and whether the 3.0 is available across all channels at launch has not been confirmed.

We will update this article as Human Touch releases full specifications and pricing.

## How It Compares

The most direct comparison point is Human Touch's own [Novo XT2](/chairs/human-touch-novo-xt2), which sits within the same brand family and serves a similar buyer profile. The Novo XT2 uses an L-track design and has been a strong seller in the sub-premium range, which means the Super Novo 3.0 needs to offer a meaningful step up to justify what will almost certainly be a higher price. Buyers who find the Novo XT2 satisfying on paper but want the expanded coverage and feature set of the flagship tier are the most natural Super Novo 3.0 customer.

Outside the Human Touch lineup, the [Luraco i9 Max](/chairs/luraco-i9-max) is worth considering for buyers attracted to American brand positioning and premium construction claims. The i9 Max competes at a similar tier and appeals to buyers who want a zero-gravity experience with strong back coverage. The Super Novo 3.0 will need to offer a compelling counter-argument on either massage feel or overall value to pull buyers away from that option.
