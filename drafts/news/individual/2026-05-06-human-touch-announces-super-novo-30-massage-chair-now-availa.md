Human Touch announced the Super Novo 3.0 massage chair is now available for purchase as of March 31, 2026, expanding the brand's premium Ascent Series lineup. The Super Novo line has been one of Human Touch's flagship offerings for several years, and this third iteration positions itself as the most advanced version yet in that lineage.

## What Was Announced

According to Human Touch, the Super Novo 3.0 is a new addition to the Ascent Series, the brand's top-tier product family. The announcement was made from Long Beach, California, where Human Touch is headquartered, and was distributed via press release on March 31, 2026. Human Touch describes itself as "a leader in premium massage chair technology and high-performance wellness," framing the Super Novo 3.0 as a continuation of that positioning.

Beyond the availability announcement and its placement within the Ascent Series, detailed specifications from the press release were limited in the source material available to us at time of publication. Specifications are pending verification.

## What This Means for Buyers

The Super Novo name carries real weight in the premium massage chair segment. The original Super Novo was one of the more recognizable chairs in the $6,000 to $9,000 price range, known for combining a 3D massage mechanism with wellness-oriented features like cloud feet reflexology and body scanning. A 3.0 revision suggests Human Touch has made meaningful updates rather than cosmetic ones, but buyers should wait for confirmed specs before assuming which specific improvements have been made.

If you are already considering the Human Touch ecosystem or have used a Super Novo at a showroom, the 3.0 is worth putting on your short list. The Ascent Series branding signals this is aimed at serious buyers looking for a long-term investment in home wellness, not an entry-level purchase.

For buyers trying to decide where the Super Novo 3.0 fits in the broader market, a few questions are worth asking. Does it use a 3D or 4D roller mechanism? What is the track type, L-track or SL-track, and how does the coverage compare to competitors at a similar price? Until those details are confirmed, it is difficult to say definitively whether this chair outperforms rivals or simply refreshes existing strengths.

If you are prioritizing full-body coverage with extended lumbar and glute reach, an [L-track chair](/l-track-massage-chairs) may serve you better depending on what the 3.0's track configuration turns out to be. If you care most about arm and shoulder work alongside back massage, the track type matters less than the mechanism quality. The source summary available to us at publication does not include confirmed pricing for the Super Novo 3.0. Human Touch's prior Super Novo models have generally sat in the upper-mid to premium range, but the 3.0's exact price point has not been independently verified here.

Specific technical specifications, including roller dimensions, airbag placement and count, weight and height range accommodations, zero-gravity recline stages, and heating zone details, are not confirmed from the available source material. Specifications are pending verification.

It is also unclear at this time whether the Super Novo 3.0 replaces the prior Super Novo model in Human Touch's lineup or runs alongside it. If it replaces the earlier version, that could affect availability and pricing of the previous model for buyers who prefer a proven option at a lower cost.
