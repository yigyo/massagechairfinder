Yanko Design, a well-regarded technology and design publication, published a hands-on impression of the Bodyfriend Quantum at IFA 2024 in Berlin. The chair carries a $15,000 price tag, placing it firmly at the top of the luxury massage chair segment. The piece is one of the few independent, in-person accounts of the chair from a credible non-industry outlet.

## What Was Announced

According to Yanko Design's hands-on report, the Bodyfriend Quantum is positioned as a robotic massage chair at the $15,000 price point. The Yanko Design writer sat in the chair at IFA 2024 and described the experience as luxurious. Bodyfriend, a South Korean brand with a strong presence in the premium segment, chose IFA, one of the world's largest consumer electronics trade shows, as the venue to showcase the Quantum to an international audience.

Specific technical specifications from the Yanko Design account are limited in what was shared in the source summary, so we are treating the $15,000 price and the "robotic massage" positioning as the two confirmed data points. Specifications beyond those are pending verification from Bodyfriend directly.

## What This Means for Buyers

A $15,000 massage chair occupies a narrow category. At that price, buyers are not just purchasing massage intensity or a long track. They are buying build quality, design language that fits a high-end living space, and the kind of session customization that typically requires input from a trained therapist. The Bodyfriend Quantum, based on how Bodyfriend has positioned its flagship products historically, appears aimed at that exact buyer.

Who is the likely buyer here? Someone who has already decided a premium massage chair belongs in their home and is comparing the absolute top of the market rather than evaluating whether to spend $3,000 or $5,000. At $15,000, this chair competes in the same conversation as the highest-tier offerings from Inada, Luraco, and select Bodyfriend models already available in North America.

For buyers with chronic back tension, shoulder tightness, or post-workout recovery needs, the relevant questions at this price tier shift away from "does it work" and toward "does it work better than chairs at half the price, and does it justify the premium." The Yanko Design hands-on suggests the Quantum clears the bar for luxury feel and in-person presence, but that is a different standard than therapeutic effectiveness measured over weeks of daily use.

If you are a buyer currently considering something in the $5,000 to $8,000 range, it is worth reading how the Quantum is described before committing, because the gap between $8,000 and $15,000 needs to be justified by something concrete. - US availability and distribution are not confirmed. Bodyfriend does operate in the United States, but whether the Quantum is currently sold through US showrooms or online is unclear from the IFA coverage alone.
- Warranty terms and after-sale service coverage at the $15,000 price point have not been detailed in available sources. At this tier, warranty and in-home service are significant factors in the true cost of ownership.

## How It Compares

At $15,000, the Bodyfriend Quantum is priced above most chairs in the MCF catalog. The closest comparisons in terms of price tier and brand positioning are chairs from [Luraco](/chairs/luraco), which manufactures in the United States and emphasizes medical-grade build quality, and [Inada](/chairs/inada), which has long set the benchmark for Japanese therapeutic engineering.

Buyers comparing the Quantum to something like the [Inada Dreamwave](/chairs/inada-dreamwave) or similar flagship-tier chairs should understand that at this price, each brand is making a different argument about what premium means. Inada emphasizes shiatsu technique and decades of refinement. Luraco emphasizes domestic manufacturing and clinical design. Bodyfriend, based on its IFA presentation, appears to emphasize robotic precision and luxury aesthetics.

None of those arguments is wrong. They reflect different buyer priorities. If your priority is a chair that disappears into a traditional living room, you will weigh those differently than if you want a chair that reads as a design object in a modern space.
