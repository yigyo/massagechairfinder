Luraco, the Texas-based manufacturer behind one of the few massage chair lines built domestically in the United States, has announced that its medical massage chairs are now officially licensed and accredited as Durable Medical Equipment (DME). The announcement was reported via Yahoo Finance and marks a significant shift in how Luraco chairs can be purchased, reimbursed, and used in clinical settings.

## What Was Announced

According to Luraco, its massage chairs have received formal DME licensing and accreditation. DME is a specific regulatory classification used by Medicare, Medicaid, and most private insurers to categorize medical devices that provide therapeutic benefit and are intended for repeated use. According to the brand, this accreditation positions Luraco chairs for purchase through medical facilities, clinical environments, and potentially through insurance-covered or reimbursable channels for qualifying patients.

Luraco has long marketed its chairs with a medical and therapeutic emphasis, and this accreditation formalizes that positioning in a way that has regulatory and financial weight.

## What This Means for Buyers

This development matters most to three distinct groups of buyers, and it is worth breaking down each case.

**Buyers with HSA or FSA accounts.** DME status strengthens the argument for using Health Savings Account (HSA) or Flexible Spending Account (FSA) funds to purchase a Luraco chair. Massage chairs have historically occupied a gray area in HSA/FSA eligibility, often requiring a letter of medical necessity from a physician. DME accreditation does not automatically guarantee reimbursement, but it gives buyers and their healthcare providers a stronger basis for submitting a claim. If you are considering this path, consult your HSA administrator and your physician before purchasing.

**Buyers seeking insurance coverage.** For individuals with documented medical conditions, including chronic back pain, fibromyalgia, or post-surgical recovery needs, DME classification opens a potential pathway for partial insurance reimbursement. The process is rarely simple and will depend on your insurer, your diagnosis, and whether your physician prescribes the chair as medically necessary. But Luraco is now one of the very few massage chair brands positioned to even attempt this conversation with an insurer.

**Medical facilities and clinical buyers.** Chiropractors, physical therapy clinics, and rehabilitation centers often face procurement requirements that demand DME-classified equipment. Luraco's accreditation makes it far easier for these buyers to justify a purchase through standard medical procurement channels.

For the typical home buyer without a specific medical diagnosis or HSA strategy, this accreditation is less directly useful in the short term. However, it does signal something meaningful about the product's construction standards and the brand's long-term commitment to the therapeutic segment of the market. If you are evaluating Luraco against a comparable luxury chair from a brand focused primarily on the home relaxation market, this distinction is worth factoring into your thinking.

Luraco chairs, including the [iRobotics 7 Plus](/chairs/luraco-irobotics-7-plus), are already among the most clinically oriented options in the [premium massage chair segment](/best-massage-chairs). The DME designation reinforces that positioning rather than creating it from scratch.

## What We Do Not Know Yet

Several important details are not yet clear from the available announcement.

First, it is not confirmed which specific Luraco models carry the DME accreditation. The announcement references "Luraco Medical Massage Chairs" broadly, but whether this applies to the full product line or a specific subset of models has not been verified.

Second, the practical reimbursement process has not been detailed. DME accreditation is a prerequisite for insurance coverage conversations, not a guarantee of coverage. The specific billing codes, required diagnoses, and insurer-by-insurer variability have not been addressed in the announcement.

Third, pricing implications, if any, have not been disclosed. DME-classified equipment sold through medical channels sometimes carries different pricing than retail. Whether Luraco will maintain consistent pricing across both channels is not yet known.

We will update this article as Luraco provides additional clarification.

## How It Compares

In the broader context of the [high-end massage chair market](/best-massage-chairs), Luraco's DME accreditation sets it apart from virtually every other brand at a similar price point.

The [Luraco iRobotics 7 Plus](/chairs/luraco-irobotics-7-plus) competes in a price range where it sits alongside chairs like the [Human Touch Novo XT2](/chairs/human-touch-novo-xt2), which is a strong performer with a well-regarded track design and a focus on the home wellness market. Human Touch has clinical partnerships and has marketed to medical professionals, but its chairs do not carry DME accreditation.

Similarly, brands like [Infinity](/best-infinity-massage-chairs) and [Osaki](/best-osaki-massage-chairs) compete on features and value in overlapping price tiers, but neither has pursued DME classification as part of their market positioning.

For a buyer who is purely focused on features per dollar, this distinction may not change the calculus. But for a buyer navigating a medical need, a clinical procurement process, or an HSA reimbursement strategy, Luraco's accreditation creates a meaningful separation from the rest of the field.

The takeaway is straightforward: if a therapeutic or medically oriented purchase is your primary motivation, Luraco has just made a stronger case for itself than any other brand currently in this category.
