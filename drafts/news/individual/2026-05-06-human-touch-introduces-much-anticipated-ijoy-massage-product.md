Human Touch has announced the relaunch and expansion of its iJOY massage product line, according to a press release distributed via GlobeNewswire. The announcement positions iJOY as a distinct product family within the Human Touch brand, though specific model names, configurations, and confirmed retail prices have not yet been independently detailed beyond the announcement itself.

## What Was Announced

According to Human Touch, the iJOY lineup represents a "much anticipated" return or expansion of the iJOY brand, which the company has previously used for entry-level and mid-range massage products. The GlobeNewswire release signals that multiple products are included in the new lineup rather than a single chair. Human Touch describes the iJOY series as a product family, suggesting differentiated tiers within the line aimed at reaching buyers at more than one price point.

Beyond the brand-level announcement, specific feature sets, track types, roller configurations, and retail pricing have not been confirmed in the source material. Specifications are pending verification.

## What This Means for Buyers

The iJOY name has historically been associated with Human Touch's more accessible price tier, generally targeting buyers who want a recognizable brand at a lower cost of entry than Human Touch's flagship models such as the [Novo XT2](/chairs/human-touch-novo-xt2) or the [Certus](/chairs/human-touch-certus). If the new lineup follows that pattern, buyers shopping in the $1,500 to $3,500 range may find the iJOY chairs worth evaluating as a starting point.

That said, this price segment is competitive. Chairs from Kahuna, Osaki, and Infinity regularly compete here, and buyers comparing options in this range should look carefully at track type, massage coverage, and warranty terms before committing. A longer S-track or L-track can make a meaningful difference for people with lower back tension or sciatica, and those details are not yet confirmed for the iJOY lineup.

For buyers who need full-body coverage including the glutes and hamstrings, an L-track or SL-track design is worth prioritizing. If the iJOY models ship with a shorter S-track, they may serve upper and mid-back relief well but fall short for full lower-body work. That distinction matters depending on why you are shopping.

Human Touch chairs have generally earned reasonable marks for build quality and their proprietary FlexGlide orbital track technology in higher-end models, but it is not yet confirmed whether any iJOY models will carry that mechanism or use a more conventional roller system.

Buyers who are brand-loyal to Human Touch and were previously considering the [Novo XT2](/chairs/human-touch-novo-xt2) at a higher price point may find the iJOY line worth a look if the pricing lands where historical iJOY products have. If the goal is maximum feature density for the dollar, the iJOY lineup will need to be evaluated against the broader [massage chair buying guide](/buying-guide) once full specs are available.

## What We Do Not Know Yet

Quite a bit remains unconfirmed at this stage:

- **Specific model names and count** within the lineup have not been detailed in available source material.
- **Track type and roller configuration** are unconfirmed. Whether these are S-track, L-track, or SL-track designs will significantly affect who these chairs are right for.
- **Availability date and retail channels** have not been specified. It is unclear whether these will be sold direct, through specialty retailers, or both.
- **Weight and height accommodation ranges** are not yet published.
- **Warranty terms** have not been confirmed.
