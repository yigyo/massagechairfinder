Ceragem has announced the Master V11 as the flagship completion of its premium massage chair lineup, according to a report in The Korea Times published in May 2026. The chair appears to represent Ceragem's most serious push yet into the upper tier of the home massage chair market, a segment where it has historically been a quieter presence compared to brands like Kyota and OHCO.

## What Was Announced

According to The Korea Times, the Ceragem Master V11 completes the brand's premium massage chair lineup, signaling that it is intended as the company's top-tier residential offering. Ceragem describes the V11 as a flagship model, though full technical specifications have not been independently published at this time. Specifications are pending verification from official Ceragem product documentation.

Ceragem is a South Korean wellness company with a long history in thermal massage therapy products, including its well-known automated thermal massage beds. The Master V11 appears to extend that heritage into the premium home massage chair category.

## What This Means for Buyers

If you are actively shopping in the [high-end massage chair segment](/reviews/best-luxury-massage-chairs), the Master V11 is worth watching for a few reasons.

First, Ceragem's background in thermal therapy suggests the V11 may incorporate heat elements more central to the massage experience than the simple lumbar heat pads found on many chairs in this price range. That would matter most to buyers dealing with chronic lower back tension or those who have found standard chair massage insufficient without thermal support.

Second, the framing of this chair as a "lineup completion" suggests it sits above existing Ceragem models in both features and price. That positioning would place it in direct conversation with chairs like the [Kyota Kokoro M888](/reviews/kyota-kokoro-m888) and the [OHCO M.8](/reviews/ohco-m8), two chairs that have set a high bar in the $7,000 to $10,000 range for body scanning precision, track coverage, and overall build quality.

Third, buyers who have been considering Ceragem's more mid-range offerings as a value alternative now have reason to look at the full lineup with fresh eyes. A credible flagship can raise the perceived quality of an entire brand. Whether the V11 delivers enough to justify premium pricing is a question we cannot answer until full specs and hands-on evaluation are available.

## How It Compares

Without confirmed specifications, any comparison is preliminary. That said, the $6,000 to $10,000 segment has clear reference points worth understanding as you track the V11's development.

The [Kyota Kokoro M888](/reviews/kyota-kokoro-m888) is among the most capable chairs in this range, offering a long SL-track, zero gravity recline, and a well-regarded body scan system. Its heat integration is functional but not the centerpiece of the experience, which could be a meaningful differentiator if Ceragem's thermal heritage translates meaningfully into the V11.

The [OHCO M.8](/reviews/ohco-m8) competes at the top of this range with an emphasis on refined build quality, smooth mechanism noise, and a more sophisticated aesthetic compared to chairs that prioritize feature count. Buyers drawn to OHCO tend to weight user experience and fit and finish heavily. If the V11 wants to compete directly with the M.8, it will need to demonstrate comparable refinement, not just comparable spec sheets.
